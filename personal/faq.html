Q. How much experience do I need to have to sign up for a track day?
A. Any and all experience levels are welcome, and newer drivers will have the option to sign up for time with a one-on-one instructor.

Q. Do I need to bring my own car?
A. Most any car will do fine on the track, but think twice about bringing a station wagon or stock VW Beetle to the track. Cars are available to rent per session or per day.

Q. What does a day at the track look like?
A. There are three run groups, A for advanced, B for high intermediate, and C for low intermediate. Starting with A group on the hour, the next group goes out every twenty minutes from 9 AM until about 5, with an hour catered lunch break at noon.

Q. How do I know what group I’ll be in?
A. When you first start off, you’ll start in C unless you have other experience and talk to one of the instructors. They can ride with you and pass you off as a B driver. Every A driver has ridden with Tom and they have all passed his very strict test.

Q. My buddy and I are sharing/renting one car, do we both have to pay for a whole day?
A. That depends. If you share a car and are running in the same run group, then only one has to pay because each is only getting half a day of driving. You can, however, run the same car in different groups to both get a full day of driving, but both would then have to register and pay for the track day.
